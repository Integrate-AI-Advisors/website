What AI Agents Actually Do (No Jargon, We Promise)

By IntegrateAI Team

You've probably heard the term "AI agents" thrown around a lot recently. Half the LinkedIn posts in your feed mention them. Your tech-savvy friend won't shut up about them. And somewhere between the hype and the confusion, you're left wondering: what do AI agents actually do, and should I care?

We work with small and medium businesses across Oxfordshire and the wider UK, helping them deploy AI agents for real, measurable outcomes. Here's what we wish someone had told us when we started.

What is an AI agent, really?

Forget the sci-fi imagery. An AI agent is a piece of software that can take instructions, make decisions, and complete tasks without you hovering over it. Think of it like hiring a very capable intern who works 24 hours a day, never calls in sick, and costs a fraction of a salary.

The key difference between an AI agent and a chatbot like ChatGPT is autonomy. When you use ChatGPT, you type a question, get an answer, and move on. An AI agent, on the other hand, takes a goal and works towards it independently. It can:

  • Research leads and compile reports while you sleep
  • Draft and send personalised outreach emails on your behalf
  • Monitor your inbox and flag priority items
  • Generate weekly marketing content based on your brand voice
  • Handle customer enquiries in your Slack channel

The word "agent" matters. These tools don't just respond to prompts. They act on your behalf, following rules you set, using information you provide.

How do AI agents work in a real business?

Let's make this concrete. Say you run a 6-person consultancy in the Cotswolds. You need leads, but nobody on your team has time to do proper prospecting. Hiring a business development person would cost you upwards of £45,000 a year.

An AI research agent could sit inside your Slack workspace and, every morning, deliver 10 qualified leads with company information, key contacts, and a suggested outreach message. It pulls data from public sources, cross-references your ideal client profile, and presents everything in a format your team can act on immediately.

That's not hypothetical. That's what our clients use every day. The agent costs a fraction of what you'd pay a junior hire, and it doesn't take holidays.

Do AI agents replace human employees?

This is the question everyone asks, and the honest answer is: sometimes yes, sometimes no.

AI agents are brilliant at tasks that are repetitive, data-heavy, and follow clear rules. Research, report generation, email drafting, data entry, scheduling, and content production are all areas where agents perform well.

They're not great at tasks that require genuine human judgement, emotional intelligence, or creative thinking that breaks new ground. Your AI agent won't comfort an upset client. It won't invent a new business model. And it won't replace the relationships that make your business work.

What AI agents do is free up your human team to focus on the work that actually requires humans. The strategy. The relationships. The decisions that move the business forward.

What does it cost to run AI agents?

This varies, but we believe in being upfront about numbers. Here's a realistic breakdown:

  • Setup cost: Anywhere from £950 for a basic configuration to £3,500+ for a full agent team deployment (that's what our implementation packages cover)
  • Monthly running costs: £50–£500 per month depending on how many agents you run and how much data they process
  • What you'd spend on the equivalent human team: £40,000–£160,000 per year in salaries alone

The maths usually speaks for itself. A small business spending £300 a month on AI agents can get output that would otherwise require two or three part-time employees.

What tools do AI agents use?

The agents we deploy for our clients are built on large language models like Claude, developed by Anthropic. We use Claude because it's reliable, handles nuanced instructions well, and respects data privacy in ways that matter for UK businesses.

Most of our agent setups connect through Slack, which means your team interacts with agents the same way they'd interact with a colleague. No special software to learn. No complicated dashboards. Just messages in channels you already use.

We also use Claude Cowork to boost individual productivity across teams. It's a different use case from agents — Cowork helps each person on your team work faster, while agents handle entire workflows autonomously.

Are AI agents safe? What about my data?

A fair concern, especially if you handle client information. Here's how we approach it:

  • Every agent setup includes clear rules about what data it can and can't access
  • We define guardrails before anything goes live
  • Client-sensitive data never goes into AI systems without explicit protocols
  • We document everything, so your team knows exactly what's happening under the hood

Data governance isn't an afterthought in our process. It's built into the foundation of every deployment.

How do I know if AI agents are right for my business?

AI agents make sense for your business if:

  • You or your team spend more than 10 hours a week on repetitive tasks
  • You need capabilities (research, outreach, content, reporting) but can't justify the salary
  • You're a small team trying to compete with companies that have 10x your headcount
  • You want consistent output that doesn't depend on someone's mood or availability

They probably aren't the right fit if you're looking for a magic wand that requires no input from your side. Agents need clear instructions, defined workflows, and someone to review their output — especially in the early weeks.

The bottom line: AI agents are employees that cost a fraction of a salary, work around the clock, and handle the tasks your team doesn't have time for. They're not magic, and they're not replacement for genuine human expertise. But for the right business, they change the economics of what's possible.
