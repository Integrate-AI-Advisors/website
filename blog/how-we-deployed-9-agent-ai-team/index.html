How We Deployed a 9-Agent AI Team for Under £500/Month

By IntegrateAI Team

We wrote about not being able to afford a marketing team and building one from AI agents instead. People wanted specifics. What agents? What do they do? How much does each one cost? Here's the full breakdown.

This is our actual agent team — the squad running our marketing, sales, content, and operations from our base in Milton under Wychwood, Oxfordshire. Everything described here is live and running as you read this.

The squad: all 9 agents

Each agent runs on Claude via Anthropic's API, integrated into our Slack workspace. They operate on schedules (some daily, some weekly) and post their output directly into dedicated Slack channels where we review and act on it.

1. Research Agent

Research Agent
Finds and qualifies potential clients. Searches public sources, cross-references against our ideal client profile, and delivers 10–15 qualified leads daily with company info, key contacts, and suggested outreach angles. Posts to #leads channel every morning at 7am.
~£25/month in API costs

2. Outreach Agent

Outreach Agent
Takes the leads from Research Agent and crafts personalised outreach emails. Each email references the prospect's business, recent activity, and specific pain points. Produces 30–40 emails per batch, ready for review and sending.
~£20/month in API costs

3. Content Strategist Agent

Content Strategist Agent
Analyses search trends, competitor content, and our existing blog performance. Produces a weekly content brief with 3–5 article ideas, each with target keywords, suggested angles, and estimated search volume. Runs every Monday morning.
~£15/month in API costs

4. Blog Writer Agent

Blog Writer Agent
Takes briefs from Content Strategist and produces full first drafts. Follows our brand voice guidelines, includes proper heading structure, and targets specified keywords naturally. Outputs 1,200–1,500 word posts that need about 30 minutes of human editing.
~£30/month in API costs

5. Social Media Agent

Social Media Agent
Creates daily social posts for LinkedIn and X. Repurposes blog content, generates standalone thought leadership pieces, and formats everything for each platform. Posts are queued in a review channel with platform-specific formatting.
~£18/month in API costs

6. SEO Agent

SEO Agent
Analyses our site structure, existing content, and search performance. Identifies keyword gaps, suggests internal linking improvements, and flags technical SEO issues. Produces a weekly report with prioritised actions.
~£12/month in API costs

7. Email Newsletter Agent

Email Newsletter Agent
Curates a weekly newsletter from our latest blog posts, industry news, and AI developments relevant to SMEs. Writes engaging subject lines (A/B variants), introduction copy, and section summaries. Ready for review every Thursday afternoon.
~£10/month in API costs

8. Competitor Intelligence Agent

Competitor Intelligence Agent
Monitors competitor websites, social profiles, and content output. Summarises what they're publishing, positioning changes, new service launches, and pricing updates. Delivers a fortnightly briefing that takes 10 minutes to read.
~£8/month in API costs

9. Reporting Agent

Reporting Agent
Pulls data from all other agents and compiles a weekly marketing dashboard. Tracks leads generated, outreach response rates, content published, social engagement, and website traffic trends. Highlights what's working and what needs attention.
~£10/month in API costs

What does this cost in total?

Here's the honest monthly breakdown:

ItemMonthly cost
All 9 agents (API / token costs)£148–220
Claude Pro subscriptions (x2 team members)£36
Slack workspace£0 (existing)
Scheduling and automation tools£40–55

Total£239–326

Even at the high end, we're well under £500/month. In practice, most months come in around £280–£310.

For context, hiring a single marketing coordinator in the Cotswolds area would cost £28,000–£35,000 per year. We're getting significantly more output for roughly £3,600 per year in total costs.

How do the agents work together?

This is where it gets interesting. The agents aren't completely independent — several feed into each other:

  • Research Agent feeds leads to Outreach Agent
  • Content Strategist feeds briefs to Blog Writer and Social Media Agent
  • Blog Writer output gets repurposed by Social Media Agent and Email Newsletter Agent
  • SEO Agent informs Content Strategist's keyword targets
  • Reporting Agent tracks output from all of them

It's a pipeline, not a collection of disconnected tools. Each agent knows its role, and the hand-offs happen automatically through our Slack channels.

What took the longest to get right?

Getting the agents to produce output that sounds like us — not like generic AI content. We spent the first week refining voice guidelines, providing examples of writing we liked and writing we didn't, and iterating on instructions until the output felt natural.

The Research Agent was the fastest to get right (data gathering is relatively straightforward). The Blog Writer took the longest because writing quality is subjective, and our brand voice has specific requirements — no buzzwords, no corporate waffle, real numbers over vague claims.

The biggest mistake we made early on was giving agents too much freedom. When we tightened their instructions and gave them clearer constraints, the output improved dramatically. More specific prompts produce better results than open-ended ones.

Could you replicate this for your business?

Yes, with two caveats.

First: you need to know what you want the agents to do before you build them. "Help with marketing" isn't specific enough. "Research 10 qualified leads per day matching these criteria and deliver them to this Slack channel at 7am" is. The more precise your brief, the better the agent performs. (We cover this process in our AI agent deployment service.)

Second: the setup requires technical knowledge. Connecting agents to APIs, configuring Slack integrations, setting up scheduling, writing effective system prompts — these aren't trivial tasks. You can learn to do them, or you can hire someone who already knows how. We built our system in about 3 weeks; a business new to AI would likely need 4–6 weeks with guidance.

The bottom line: 9 agents, under £500/month, producing the output of a 3–4 person marketing team. It took 3 weeks to set up, about 45 minutes a day to review and direct, and it runs 24/7 from our small office in Oxfordshire. If you run a small business and you've been putting off marketing because you can't afford to hire, this is a genuine alternative worth considering.
