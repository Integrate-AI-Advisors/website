Can't Afford a Marketing Team? Here's What We Did Instead

By IntegrateAI Team

Here's the uncomfortable truth about running a small consultancy: you know you need marketing, sales development, and content production. You also know that hiring people for those roles would cost £120,000–£180,000 a year in salaries alone. And you definitely don't have that budget.

We know because we're in the same position. IntegrateAI Advisors is a small AI consultancy based in Milton under Wychwood, Oxfordshire. We practise what we preach. We couldn't afford a marketing team, so we built one from AI agents.

This is the honest story of what that looks like.

What were we actually spending our time on?

Before we set up our agent team, a typical week looked something like this:

  • 8–10 hours researching potential clients and compiling outreach lists
  • 5–6 hours writing blog posts, social media content, and email newsletters
  • 3–4 hours drafting and personalising outreach emails
  • 2–3 hours pulling together weekly reports and tracking metrics
  • 2–3 hours on SEO research, keyword analysis, and website updates

That's roughly 20–26 hours a week on tasks that, while important, kept us from doing the actual client work we get paid for. Every hour spent writing a LinkedIn post was an hour not spent deploying AI for a client.

What does our AI agent team actually produce?

We now run 9 AI agents that handle the bulk of this work. Here's what they deliver in a typical week:

  • 50+ qualified leads researched and delivered with company details, key contacts, and suggested outreach angles
  • 200+ personalised outreach emails drafted and ready for review
  • 3–4 blog posts at varying stages of production (research, first draft, editing pass)
  • Daily social media content across LinkedIn and X, scheduled and formatted
  • Weekly marketing performance report with metrics, trends, and suggested actions
  • SEO analysis with keyword opportunities and content gap reports

None of this goes out without human review. We check everything, edit what needs editing, and reject anything that doesn't meet our standards. But the point is that the heavy lifting — the research, the drafting, the data compilation — happens overnight, every night, without us doing any of it.

We're getting the output of a 3–4 person marketing team for less than £5,000 a year in running costs.

What about quality? Is it actually any good?

Here's where we need to be straight with you. AI-generated content ranges from "surprisingly good" to "needs significant editing." On average, we'd say:

  • Research and data compilation: 90% ready to use. The agents are excellent at gathering, organising, and summarising information
  • Outreach emails: 80% ready. They need a human eye to catch tone issues and add personal touches, but the structure and personalisation are solid
  • Blog content: 60–70% ready. Good structure and research, but the writing often needs editing to match our voice and add genuine insight. (This post, for context, was written by a human)
  • Reports and analytics: 95% ready. This is where agents genuinely excel — pulling data, formatting it, and highlighting what matters

The quality gap shrinks over time as you refine the agents' instructions and build up a library of examples they can learn from. Our agents today produce noticeably better output than they did three months ago.

What's the catch? There has to be a catch

There are several, and we'd be doing you a disservice by not mentioning them:

Setup takes time and expertise. Getting 9 agents configured, tested, and reliably producing good output took us about 3 weeks of focused work. We already knew what we were doing. If you're starting from scratch, expect it to take longer — or get help from someone who's done it before.

You still need humans. Every piece of content needs review. Every outreach email needs a final check. The agents handle the production; your team handles the quality control and strategic direction. If you're a solo founder, this still saves you 15–20 hours a week. But it doesn't save you all of them.

Agents make mistakes. They occasionally get facts wrong, misunderstand context, or produce something that sounds robotic. You need processes to catch these before anything goes public. We review everything in batches each morning — it takes about 45 minutes.

Running costs aren't zero. £300–£500 a month is real money for a small business. It's dramatically less than hiring, but it's not free. And the costs scale with usage — more agents doing more work means higher API bills.

Would this work for your business?

This approach works best if you:

  • Run a small business (1–20 people) and need marketing output you can't afford to hire for
  • Have clearly defined tasks — research, writing, outreach, reporting — that eat up your week
  • Are willing to spend 30–60 minutes a day reviewing and directing agent output
  • Can invest in proper setup (either your own time or professional help)

It works less well if you need highly creative or strategic marketing that requires deep human insight. Agents won't develop your brand positioning or come up with a new go-to-market strategy. They execute. You direct.

Honest summary: We went from spending 20+ hours a week on marketing tasks to about 5, while producing significantly more output. The cost went from "we can't afford to hire anyone" to "under £500/month." It's not perfect, and it requires daily involvement. But for a small business that needs marketing capability without marketing budgets, this is genuinely worth exploring.
